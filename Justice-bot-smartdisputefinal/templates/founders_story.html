A Mother's Fight for Justice

Seven years ago, my world changed forever when the Children's Aid Society took my children. What followed was a four-year legal battle that taught me everything about Canada's family court system - the hard way.

The System That Wasn't Built for People Like Me

I quickly learned that our legal system operates on privilege. If you can afford a $500-per-hour lawyer, you get justice. If you can't, you get form letters, bureaucratic runaround, and judges who seem to have already made up their minds.

I spent countless nights learning legal procedures, reading Charter cases, and filing my own motions. I became my own advocate because I had no choice. And slowly, I started winning.

Charter Rights Aren't Just Words on Paper

"Everyone has the right to life, liberty and security of the person and the right not to be deprived thereof except in accordance with the principles of fundamental justice."

Charter Section 7 - The foundation of my legal strategy

I discovered that Charter arguments work. When you know how to use them. When you understand that government actors - including CAS workers and family court judges - are bound by constitutional law.

The Platform That Changes Everything

After getting my children back, I realized something: if a single mom with no legal training could learn to navigate this system successfully, imagine what we could do with AI to help others.

SmartDispute.ai isn't just a legal tech platform. It's a movement to democratize access to justice in Canada. It's built specifically for people who can't afford lawyers but refuse to give up their rights.
